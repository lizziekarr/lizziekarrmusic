When I was small, I made up songs. Then I usually realized that Michael Jackson or Amy Grant had already written that melody, and I wondered if every song in the world had already been written.

In summer, I made up dance routines in the front yard, and directed skits with the kids on the street. School was a breeze, but dance class was hard. I felt shy in there, a little bit like a nobody, but a nobody who wanted to try and try.

Senior year of high school, I took twelve dance classes a week. I remember the day that my body submitted itself completely to the movement. Thought left my head. I experienced God. Since then I've vascillated between the euphoria of losing myself in movement and music and the adrenalin-charged terror of being seen and heard.

I write songs with love and madness. They are strange creatures in a ridiculous world. All the love in the world to you. I hope you can catch some of this wave that I'm riding.
